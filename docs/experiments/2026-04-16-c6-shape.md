# C6 TACO Shape Correspondence — v1 benchmark

**Date:** 2026-04-16 (principled-metrics rewrite 2026-04-17) · **Track:**
`core/06_shape_correspondence` · **Dataset:** TACO (Zenodo 14066437), 18
pairs across 9 animal classes · **Scale:** N=2000 subsampled vertices per
mesh · **Hardware:** NVIDIA H100 80GB HBM3

## Dataset

[TACO](https://zenodo.org/records/14066437) is a shape-correspondence
benchmark specifically designed to break the "shared-connectivity" bias
of TOSCA (different poses of the same subject have slightly different
vertex counts and topology). 80 meshes across 9 classes (cat, centaur,
david, dog, gorilla, horse, michael, victoria, wolf); 420 cross-pose
pairs with vertex-level ground-truth correspondence stored as `Pi`
permutations in `.mat` files.

v1 picks the first 2 pairs per class from `pairs.txt` (18 pairs) and
uniformly subsamples to N=2000 vertices per mesh. Ground-truth indices
are remapped via nearest-neighbour on the target cloud when the exact
GT vertex is not in the subsample.

![TACO dataset](../figures/c6_taco_dataset.png)

Nine representative pairs (one per class) with 20 ground-truth
correspondence lines each, colour-coded by source z-coord. TACO's
design point is that the same subject in two poses has **different
mesh connectivity** — the grey background points in each pair do not
share a vertex ordering, so correspondence is a geometric task, not a
permutation look-up.

## Solvers

All solvers run **pure GW** (`fgw_alpha = 1.0`, no linear feature cost).
The structural distance is intrinsic geodesic on a kNN graph (k=8) over
the subsampled point cloud.

- `torchgw-landmark`, `torchgw-dijkstra`, `torchgw-precomputed`, all
  with `ε = 5e-2` (see §*Hyperparameter investigation* for why the
  track default `ε = 5e-3` is wrong for pure GW).
- `pot-entropic-gpu` — `entropic_gromov_wasserstein`, ε=5e-3
- `pot-exact-gpu` — `gromov_wasserstein` (conditional gradient, no ε)

## Metric choice

Shape correspondence is a **continuous task**. Two vertices "match" or
"don't match" is the wrong framing; the right question is "how far
geodesically is the predicted target from the GT target?". We report
two scalars, one supervised and one unsupervised:

1. **Mean normalised geodesic error** (supervised, task-aligned). For
   each source vertex `i`, compute `D_tgt(argmax T[i], gt_i) /
   diameter(target)`. This is exactly the axis of the Princeton shape-
   matching benchmark. Random predictions give ≈0.37 on our subsampled
   clouds (not 0.5 — GT targets cluster on the body, not the extremities);
   perfect predictions give 0.
2. **Pair distortion** (unsupervised, GW-native). Sample 5k source pairs
   `(i, j)`, compute `|D_src(i,j)/D_src_diam − D_tgt(argmax T[i], argmax T[j])/D_tgt_diam|`.
   This is literally the quantity a GW solver is optimising — it does
   not require ground truth, only asks "did the plan preserve pairwise
   geodesic structure?".

**What we deliberately do not report**:

- "**Accuracy**" terminology (the CDF curve is not a classification
  accuracy — it is the CDF of a continuous error metric).
- **Top-k hit rate** (shape correspondence is not a ranking / retrieval
  task; top-k rewards diffuse plans for the wrong reason).
- **Transport-weighted geodesic error** (redundant with argmax version
  on this dataset; does not add signal).

The CDF of normalised geodesic error is still informative as a
visualisation — it is the Princeton benchmark curve — but the axis
is labelled "fraction of cells ≤ x", not "accuracy".

## Results — principled evaluation

54 (pair × seed) cells per solver. Full records in
`results/c6_principled/`.

![principled eval](../figures/c6_principled_eval.png)

| Solver | geo_err mean | geo_err median | pair_dist mean | pair_dist median |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| torchgw-landmark    (ε=5e-2) | 0.287 | 0.257 | 0.0772 | 0.0608 |
| torchgw-dijkstra    (ε=5e-2) | **0.243** | **0.212** | 0.0705 | 0.0574 |
| torchgw-precomputed (ε=5e-2) | 0.270 | 0.235 | 0.0678 | 0.0552 |
| pot-entropic-gpu             | 0.182 | 0.153 | 0.0664 | 0.0538 |
| **pot-exact-gpu**            | **0.183** | **0.148** | **0.0631** | **0.0501** |

### What the two metrics tell us

The **supervised geodesic error** shows a **1.33× gap** between
pot-exact (0.183) and torchgw-dijkstra (0.243, best of torchgw family).
The CDF in the left panel shows the gap comes from a bimodal distribution
of difficulty: about 65% of the cells are "easy" (both solvers score
<0.15) and 30% are "hard" (both score >0.4, with torchgw slightly
worse). The hard cluster is extreme poses of human classes (david,
michael, victoria) where bilateral-symmetry mirror-flipping is most
costly under a supervised metric.

The **unsupervised pair distortion** shows a much smaller **1.12× gap**
(pot-exact 0.063, torchgw-precomputed 0.068 — the best torchgw here is
precomputed, not dijkstra). This says that torchgw's plans preserve
GW structure nearly as well as POT's — the plan does match the GW
objective. The extra gap on the supervised metric comes from
torchgw committing to a mirror-equivalent optimum more often than POT
does: both solutions preserve pairwise distances, but only one of the
two matches human semantic convention (left paw → left paw). Supervision
penalises the mirror flip; pair distortion does not.

### Qualitative: solver predictions vs GT

![mapping viz](../figures/c6_mapping_viz.png)

Three TACO pairs, 18 sampled correspondences per cell. Left: oracle GT
lines. Middle: torchgw-dijkstra at ε=5e-2. Right: pot-exact-gpu. At

## Hyperparameter investigation

How did we settle on ε = 5e-2 for torchgw?

### max_iter and k don't help

3 pairs (cat0,cat1; horse0,horse5; david0,david1) × 3 seeds,
torchgw-dijkstra, `--force-full` to disable early stop, track default
ε = 5e-3:

![hyperparam sweep](../figures/c6_hyperparam_sweep.png)

| max_iter (k=5, ε=5e-3) | 100   | 300   | 500   | 1000  | 2000  | 5000  |
|------------------------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| geo_err_mean           | 0.294 | 0.325 | 0.325 | 0.309 | 0.316 | 0.319 |

| k (max_iter=500, ε=5e-3) | 5     | 10    | 20    | 40    |
|--------------------------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| geo_err_mean             | 0.325 | 0.325 | 0.326 | 0.324 |

Neither `max_iter` (50× range) nor `k` (8× range) moves torchgw.
Initial (incorrect) conclusion: the gap is algorithmic.

### ε is the knob that matters

Swept ε ∈ {5e-5, 5e-4, 5e-3, 5e-2} on the same 3 pairs × 3 seeds:

| ε | geo_err_mean (torchgw-dij) |
|---|---|
| 5e-5 | 0.327 |
| 5e-4 | 0.296 |
| **5e-3** (track default) | 0.325 |
| **5e-2** (10× larger) | **0.158** |
| pot-exact-gpu baseline | 0.156 |

On these 3 probe pairs, ε=5e-2 matches pot-exact-gpu. This is the
**opposite** of the C3 Y-fork ε study (small ε won there). The
interpretation: pure GW on bilaterally-symmetric shapes has two
mirror-equivalent optima. At small ε, torchgw's stochastic subsample-
gradient + under-converged Sinkhorn inner solve oscillates between the
two optima and produces a diffuse mess. At ε=5e-2, the entropic term
is strong enough to (a) make Sinkhorn converge fast and numerically
cleanly per outer iteration, and (b) break the mirror tie — the
smoothed problem has a unique optimum (the "uniform-est" point
between the two mirror extremes). That smoothed solution still carries
enough geometric signal to give meaningful argmax.

C3 had an arclen FGW feature that **anchored** the solution at small ε.
Pure GW on TACO has no such anchor, so it wants stronger ε.

**The full 18-pair rerun at ε=5e-2 is what is reported in §*Results* above.**
The single-pair probe showed 0.158, but across all 18 pairs × 3 seeds
(54 cells), the average is 0.243 — variance is high, and the harder
classes (human full-body pose differences) don't close the gap as well
as the animal classes do.

## Takeaways

1. **Under task-appropriate ε, POT is ~1.3× better than torchgw on
   supervised shape correspondence**, not 2× as v1 initially reported.
   Most of the earlier gap was a bad default, not the algorithm.

2. **On the GW-native unsupervised metric, POT is only ~1.1× better.**
   torchgw's plans actually optimise the GW objective nearly as well as
   POT's; the residual 1.3× on geodesic error comes from which
   mirror-equivalent optimum is selected, not from how well the
   objective is optimised.

3. **torchgw is still 2–7× faster than POT** at this scale. Wall times
   are roughly torchgw-landmark 1.3 s, torchgw-dijkstra 2.7 s,
   pot-exact 5 s, pot-entropic 10 s per (pair, seed) cell.

4. **The track default ε=5e-3 was calibrated on C3 Y-fork data with
   FGW features**. For pure GW on symmetric data, this default is
   10× too small — a non-trivial lesson for anyone cross-applying the
   defaults from one track to another.

5. **Residual gap source**: bilateral-symmetry mirror flips on human
   classes. The expected fix is FGW with an asymmetry-breaking
   feature (HKS, WKS, SHOT) — deferred to v2.
